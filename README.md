Abstract
The Freelancer Management System is a web and mobile-based platform designed to streamline the interaction between clients and freelancers, enabling efficient project management, communication, and payment handling. The system provides three main user interfaces ā€” Admin, Client, and Freelancer ā€” each with specific roles and functionalities to ensure smooth workflow and transparency.
The Admin module manages user verification and oversees the overall platform operations. Administrators can verify registered clients and freelancers, handle user complaints by sending replies, and view freelancer reviews to maintain service quality.
The Client module allows users to register, log in, and manage their profiles. Clients can post, edit, and delete job listings, communicate directly with freelancers via an integrated chat system, monitor the progress of their work, make secure payments, and provide feedback and ratings for freelancers based on their performance.
The Freelancer module, developed using Flutter and Dart, enables freelancers to register, log in, browse available jobs, and send job requests. Freelancers can track their job request status, update their work progress through daily reports, engage in chat with clients, view their payment details, and manage complaints and replies from the admin.
This system leverages Django for the backend and MySQL for data storage, ensuring data integrity and seamless communication between modules. The integration of both web (client/admin) and mobile (freelancer) platforms offers a convenient and efficient environment for managing freelance projects, promoting trust, accountability, and productivity between clients and freelancers.

Existing System
In the existing system, most interactions between clients and freelancers are handled through manual communication methods or through third-party freelance websites that lack full transparency and control. Clients often rely on social media platforms, emails, or informal websites to find freelancers, which makes the process inefficient and prone to misunderstandings.
There is no centralized system to manage the complete workflow ā€” from job posting and freelancer selection to payment and performance review. Clients have to contact freelancers individually, negotiate work terms manually, and track project progress through personal communication, which can lead to confusion, delays, and miscommunication.
Similarly, freelancers struggle to find suitable jobs that match their skills and often face issues like delayed payments, lack of proper job tracking, and limited opportunities for feedback. There is no proper mechanism to submit progress reports or communicate formally with clients within a secure platform.
Admins, if any, have limited visibility into user activities and cannot effectively handle complaints, verify user authenticity, or maintain quality control. This increases the chances of fraud, poor service quality, and lack of accountability.
